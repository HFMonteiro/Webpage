Research Interests

Digital health, data and public health systems with a practical purpose

My research looks at how digital technology and data can support public health and health services. My doctoral work in Health Data Science covers applications in operations, surveillance, planning and outcome measurement.

Primary Research Areas

Digital Health Systems

Implementation of digital health technologies in clinical and public health settings, with attention to integration, usability, adoption and measurable impact.

  • Health Information Systems
  • Electronic Health Records
  • Telemedicine Platforms
  • Digital Health Workflows

Screening Programmes

Design and evaluation of screening programmes, including implementation, effectiveness and the use of health data for monitoring.

  • Population Health Screening
  • Program Effectiveness
  • Health Outcomes Research
  • Risk Stratification

Health Information Systems

Design and implementation of health information systems that support clinical and operational decisions, with a focus on interoperability, data quality and usability.

  • Health Data Integration
  • System Interoperability
  • Data Quality Management
  • Clinical Decision Support

Data Visualization & Business Intelligence

Data visualisation and business intelligence for health-service decisions, including dashboard design, analytical workflows and user adoption.

  • Healthcare Dashboards
  • Performance Analytics
  • Visual Analytics
  • Decision Support Systems

Current Research Focus

PhD Research: Health Data Science

Doctoral research on data analysis and machine-learning applications in public health systems, focused on population health and service improvement.

Digital Health Implementation

Research on digital health implementation in Portuguese health services, including impact measurement, adoption and system integration.

WHO/Europe Consultation

Contribution to WHO/Europe initiatives on digital health policy and implementation in European health systems.

Research Collaboration

I consider research collaborations when the technical scope, institutional setting and data governance are clear. Relevant areas include:

  • Multicentre implementation studies of digital health technologies
  • International comparative research on health-system digitalisation
  • Interdisciplinary projects combining clinical expertise with data science
  • Policy research on digital health regulation and governance

These are examples rather than a closed list. A useful proposal should explain the question, practical value, partners and data-governance arrangements.
