Research Interests

Research at the intersection of public health intelligence, digital health systems, and operational analytics

The research focuses on how digital health, health information systems, and analytics can support public health action across the portfolio: detecting public health events and emergencies, planning services around territorial and population needs, and improving programmes and operations through screening analytics, process mining, and dashboards.

Primary Research Areas

Screening and Process Mining

Studying population-based screening programmes through pathway analysis, data quality assessment, and process mining methods. This work focuses on understanding programme flows, identifying bottlenecks, and supporting operational improvement in screening services.

  • Population-Based Screening
  • Process Mining
  • Programme Pathway Analytics
  • Operational Dashboards

Public Health Emergency Information Systems / ERIMS

Exploring information systems that support public health emergency preparedness, response, and management, including ERIMS-related assessment and information-for-action approaches for surveillance and emergency coordination.

  • Emergency Information Systems
  • ERIMS
  • Public Health Surveillance
  • Information for Action

Digital Health and Health Information Systems

Examining the design, implementation, and governance of digital health and health information systems that support public health management, service planning, and clinical or operational decision-making.

  • Health Information Systems
  • Digital Health Workflows
  • Data Governance
  • Interoperability

Population Health Analytics

Using real-world data, territorial intelligence, and population health methods to describe needs, monitor patterns, and inform planning across public health and health service contexts.

  • Population Needs Assessment
  • Territorial Intelligence
  • Real-World Data
  • Risk and Service Planning

Connection to the Portfolio

Detect

Surveillance, public health emergency information, and ERIMS-related work support earlier detection, shared situational awareness, and more structured emergency response information.

Plan

Territorial intelligence, population needs assessment, and health information systems support service planning and priority-setting grounded in available data.

Improve

Screening analytics, process mining, and operational dashboards support continuous programme review, pathway visibility, and practical improvement work.

Research Collaboration

Collaboration on digital health research projects can be considered when there is a clear technical, institutional, and data-governance fit. Relevant areas include:

  • Multi-site implementation studies of digital health technologies
  • International comparative research on health system digitalization
  • Interdisciplinary projects combining clinical expertise with data science
  • Policy research on digital health regulation and governance

These areas are examples; proposals with clear objectives, practical usefulness and responsible data governance are welcome.
