Featured Work

Three larger case-study cards highlight applied public-health and digital-transformation work described at a public, aggregate level.

01

Surveillance demonstrator

Episignal Public-Health Signal Review Prototype

A governed prototype for structuring public-health signals so teams can review, interpret and prioritise information without exposing personal data.

Problem
Signals from surveillance, operations and contextual information can be hard to compare when they arrive through fragmented formats.
Context
Public-health teams need public, aggregate and reviewable views that support situational awareness while preserving caution around sensitive information.
My role
Helped structure the problem, information model, review logic and communication approach for a demonstrator.
Methods/tools
Indicator framing, workflow mapping, dashboard concepts, data-quality checks and human-in-the-loop review patterns.
Governance and human review or data protection
Designed around aggregate information, traceable assumptions and human review before interpretation or escalation.
Output
A prototype that creates visibility over signals and provides a basis for discussion and decision-making.
What it demonstrates
How governed digital tools can support surveillance work without replacing professional judgement.
Transferability
The same pattern can support other population-health monitoring topics that require transparent review and careful interpretation.
Surveillance Signals Governance
02

Planning and population health

ULS / PLS Digital Planning Demonstrator

A digital planning demonstrator that helps structure local-health evidence, priorities and review points for population-health planning.

Problem
Planning work can depend on dispersed indicators, documents and local interpretations that are difficult to keep aligned.
Context
Local Health Units and Local Health Plans require a shared evidence base that remains explainable and safe to discuss publicly at aggregate level.
My role
Supported the framing of planning needs, indicator organisation, stakeholder questions and practical outputs.
Methods/tools
Population-health indicators, territory profiles, planning logic, data visualisation and structured documentation.
Governance and human review or data protection
Uses aggregate information, avoids personal identifiers and keeps interpretation reviewable by accountable professionals.
Output
A demonstrator that supports planning conversations and provides a basis for decision-making without claiming automated prioritisation.
What it demonstrates
How public-health planning can become more transparent when evidence, assumptions and decisions are organised together.
Transferability
The approach can be adapted to other municipal, regional or programme-planning contexts where evidence needs to be reviewed collaboratively.
Public health Planning Indicators
03

Programme monitoring

Screening Programme Dashboards

Monitoring dashboard prototypes that create visibility over screening programmes and help structure review, prioritisation and follow-up.

Problem
Screening programmes need consistent ways to follow status, exceptions and operational priorities across time.
Context
Programme monitoring benefits from clear views that can be discussed by public-health and operational teams without exposing identifiable data.
My role
Helped translate programme questions into monitoring views, indicators, review routines and safe communication formats.
Methods/tools
BI dashboard design, indicator definitions, prioritisation logic, exception lists and data-quality checks.
Governance and human review or data protection
Designed for aggregate or role-appropriate review, with human interpretation of exceptions and no claim of automated decision-making.
Output
Dashboard prototypes that support recurring programme review and provide a basis for decision-making.
What it demonstrates
How data visualisation can help teams see programme status and structure operational conversations.
Transferability
The monitoring pattern can be reused for other preventive, population-health or pathway programmes.
