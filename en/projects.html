Surveillance and signal detection

EpiSignal — Surveillance and Signal Detection

A public health intelligence prototype/demonstrator for structuring data-to-signal workflows, prioritisation, uncertainty, alerting concepts and human review.

Problem
Surveillance teams often need to interpret fragmented routine data and observations before deciding what deserves review, escalation or monitoring.
Context
Public health surveillance, epidemic intelligence, operational alerting, uncertainty and review workflows.
My role
Framing the intelligence workflow as a prototype/demonstrator, with emphasis on safe interpretation and operational usefulness.
Methods/tools
Data-to-signal logic, prioritisation criteria, review queues, alerting concepts and dashboard/reporting views where appropriate.
Governance and human review
Human-in-the-loop review, traceability, privacy boundaries, uncertainty handling and accountable escalation are treated as design requirements.
Output
A reviewable public-health intelligence concept that helps structure how signals are detected, prioritised and discussed.
What it demonstrates
The ability to translate surveillance information into governed, operationally useful intelligence without claiming production use.
Transferability
Relevant to surveillance units, local public-health teams, epidemic intelligence cells and emergency preparedness workflows.

Territorial public health intelligence

ULS PLS Digital / Municipal Health Planning

Digital Local Health Plan and municipal planning work that helps structure indicators, population needs, local priorities and planning workflows.

Problem
Local planning depends on coherent population evidence, but indicators, territorial needs and decision cycles can be difficult to align.
Context
Health situation diagnosis, municipal health planning, ULS decision support and public-health planning workflows.
My role
Helping structure indicators, interpretation, planning logic and decision-support views for territorial public-health discussion.
Methods/tools
Indicator organisation, population-needs framing, territorial analysis, dashboard concepts and planning-oriented documentation.
Governance and data protection
Outputs are kept public-safe and aggregate, with clear interpretation boundaries and attention to data protection requirements.
Output
A clearer working basis for municipal and ULS planning discussions without exposing personal or sensitive information.
What it demonstrates
Territorial public-health intelligence: turning indicators into a governed basis for local priorities and planning decisions.
Transferability
Applicable to local health plans, municipal health strategies, ULS planning cycles and population-health prioritisation.
PlanningIndicatorsMunicipal healthDecision support
Improve

Screening programme intelligence

Screening Dashboards — Programme Intelligence

Operational BI and dashboard work for screening programme monitoring, prioritisation, exception tracking and data-informed improvement.

Problem
Screening programmes need visibility over participation, workflow delays, exceptions and follow-up so teams can prioritise action.
Context
Programme monitoring, especially cancer and colorectal screening work where supported by public research outputs.
My role
BI/dashboard design, process interpretation, prioritisation logic and connection to research on screening analytics and process mining.
Methods/tools
Dashboards, programme indicators, process-mining concepts, exception tracking and operational monitoring views.
Governance and data protection
Aggregate and privacy-aware monitoring, with attention to appropriate access, interpretation and responsible use of programme data.
Output
Monitoring views that create operational visibility and provide a basis for programme review and improvement conversations.
What it demonstrates
The translation of screening data into practical operational intelligence, linked to published work on screening and process mining.
Transferability
Relevant to population screening programmes, monitoring units, quality-improvement teams and healthcare BI teams.
